EN 16798 Cooling System Implementation Audit

Audit map for the cooling-side modules added around the heat-pump example: EN 16798-9 operating conditions, EN 16798-15 cooling storage and EN 16798-13 cooling generation.

Scope And Decision

EN 15316-2 And EN 15316-3

Cooling emission and water-based cooling distribution were already present in EmissionSystemCalculator and DistributionSystemCalculator. The example chain now explicitly feeds the cooling-side outputs of those modules into the EN 16798 cooling modules.

EN 16798 Integration

The attached standards are relevant because EN 15316-4-2 is a heating and DHW heat-pump generation method. Space cooling is now handled by EN 16798-9, EN 16798-15 and EN 16798-13 by default, while the earlier reversible heat-pump cooling calculation remains available as a simplified comparison path.

Implemented Cooling Boundary

The default examples model a water-based room cooling system, chilled-water buffer storage and an air-to-water compression cooling generator using EER/capacity product-map data.

Default Example Chain

The full path is the default. It keeps the EN 15316 modules for room emission, water distribution and heating/DHW storage, then uses EN 16798 for cooling operating conditions, cooling storage and cooling generation. 

ISO 52016 sensible heating/cooling needs
  -> EN 15316-2 emission effects for heating and cooling
  -> DHW profile
  -> EN 16798-9 cooling operating conditions
  -> EN 15316-3 heating/cooling/DHW distribution
  -> EN 15316-5 heating/DHW storage
  -> EN 16798-15 cooling storage
  -> EN 16798-13 cooling generation
  -> EN 15316-4-2 heating/DHW heat-pump generation
In the default path, cooling electricity is reported by CoolingGenerationSystemCalculator. Heating and DHW electricity are still reported by HeatPumpSystemCalculator. The example writes combined_generation_summary.csv so the inspection plots and annual cards show one combined generator view.

Main Calculation Formulas

EN 16798-9 Cooling Operating Conditions

Code: CoolingSystemCalculator._distribution_flow_temperature()

CONST: theta_C_dis_in_flw_req = theta_C_dis_flw_set
ODA_COMP: theta_C_dis_in_flw_req = clip(f_e * theta_e + delta_theta_off, theta_min, theta_max)

Code: CoolingSystemCalculator._volume_flow() and _return_temperature()

variable flow: q_V_C_dis = q_V_C_dis_des * Phi_C / Phi_C_des
fallback flow: q_V_C_dis = Q_C / (1.16 * DeltaT_des * t)
theta_C_dis_out_ret_req = theta_C_dis_in_flw_req + Q_C / (1.16 * q_V_C_dis * t)

Code: CoolingSystemCalculator._simulate()

Q_C_gen_in_req = Q_C_dis_out_tot_req + Q_C_dis_ls_simplified + f_wat_C_aux_dis * W_C_aux_dis_simplified

EN 16798-15 Cooling Storage

Code: CoolingStorageSystemCalculator._simulate()

Q_C_sto_out_ls = H_C_sto_out_ls * max(theta_amb - theta_C_gen_out, 0) * t / 1000
Q_C_sto_in_ls = H_C_sto_in_ls * max(theta_amb - theta_C_sto_out, 0) * t / 1000
Q_C_sto_ls = H_C_sto_tot_ls * max(theta_amb - theta_C_sto, 0) * t / 1000
Q_C_sto_ls_tot = Q_C_sto_out_ls + Q_C_sto_in_ls + Q_C_sto_ls

Code: CoolingStorageSystemCalculator._pump_runtime()

t_pump = min(Q_C / (1.16 * q_V_pump * DeltaT_pump), t_step)
W_C_sto_aux = t_pump_in * P_pump_in + t_pump_out * P_pump_out
Q_C_sto_in = Q_C_sto_out + Q_C_sto_ls_tot + W_C_sto_aux_to_medium

EN 16798-13 Cooling Generation

Code: CoolingGenerationSystemCalculator._simulate() and _capacity_and_eer()

theta_C_gen_out = max(theta_C_gen_out_req, theta_C_gen_out_limit)
Q_C_gen_in_max = capacity_kW(theta_cond_in, theta_C_gen_out) * t * f_op_C
Q_C_gen_in = Q_C_free + Q_C_mechanical + Q_C_backup
f_C_PL = Q_C_mechanical / Q_C_gen_in_max
E_C_gen_el_in = Q_C_mechanical / EER(theta_cond_in, theta_C_gen_out)
Q_C_gen_out = Q_C_gen_in + E_C_gen_el_in
E_C_total = E_C_gen_el_in + E_C_backup_in + W_C_aux_gen
SEER_C_gen = Q_C_gen_in / E_C_total

Known Limits And Audit Notes

  • EN 16798-9 is implemented as the connector for water-based cooling operating temperatures and flow; detailed room emission remains in EN 15316-2 and pipe/pump distribution remains in EN 15316-3.
  • EN 16798-15 is implemented for chilled-water buffer storage with steady hourly heat gains. Ice and PCM latent state tracking are not modelled in this first implementation.
  • EN 16798-13 currently implements compression cooling generation. Absorption, adsorption, desiccant and evaporative cooling generators are outside the implemented scope.
  • Example defaults are realistic demonstration assumptions for 120 m2 residential cases. Project-grade studies should replace them with project geometry, pump data, storage product data, national annex values and manufacturer performance maps.
  • The older reversible heat-pump cooling branch remains available through --calculation-path heat-pump-cooling or --cooling-generation-method heat-pump-simple for comparison with previous outputs.
The standards use module boundaries and sign conventions that can look surprising in a building-energy time series. In these cooling modules, positive Q_C means useful cooling removed from the space or system, while recoverable heat gains on the cooling side are reported with negative sign where the standard applies that convention.
