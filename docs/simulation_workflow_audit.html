Heat-Pump Example Simulation Workflow Audit

This page is the parent audit trail for the Athens and Bolzano heat-pump examples. It shows how the implemented standards connect to one another, which files carry data between modules, and where to continue the detailed clause-by-clause review.

Default Full Workflow

Example building geometry and schedules
  -> EN ISO 52016-1 sensible heating/cooling needs
  -> EN 15316-2 room emission and control effects for heating/cooling
  -> EN 12831-3 DHW energy-need profile and DHW design sizing
  -> EN 16798-9 cooling operating conditions and chilled-water handoff
  -> EN 15316-3 heating/cooling/DHW water distribution losses and pump auxiliaries
  -> EN 15316-5 heating/DHW storage losses and storage auxiliaries
  -> EN 16798-15 cooling storage heat gains and storage auxiliaries
  -> EN 14511 / EN 14825 product capacity, COP/EER and part-load performance data
  -> EN 15316-4-2 heating/DHW heat-pump generation
  -> EN 16798-13 cooling generation
  -> combined generator summaries, CSV outputs and HTML inspection plots
Default calculation path: python examples/heat_pump_15316_4_2_example.py --scenario athens --calculation-path full. Replace athens with bolzano for the Italian example.

Calculation Path Switches

CLI OptionWorkflow Effect
--calculation-path fullRuns the full default chain shown above.
--calculation-path simpleBypasses EN 15316-2, EN 15316-3, EN 15316-5 and EN 16798 modules; sends direct ISO 52016/DHW loads to the heat-pump calculator.
--calculation-path emission-distributionRuns EN 15316-2 and EN 15316-3, but bypasses heating/DHW storage and cooling storage.
--calculation-path storage-onlyRuns EN 15316-5 between direct ISO 52016/DHW loads and generation.
--calculation-path no-cooling-storageBypasses EN 16798-15 while retaining EN 16798-13 cooling generation.
--calculation-path heat-pump-coolingUses the older reversible heat-pump cooling branch instead of EN 16798-13.
--performance-data-method en14511-14825Builds product maps from rating points and applies EN 14825 part-load correction.
--performance-data-method simpleUses the earlier synthetic performance maps without EN 14825 correction.
--dhw-design-method en12831-3Runs the EN 12831-3 DHW design-heat-load/supply-curve sizing layer; this is the default except in the simple calculation path.
--dhw-design-method simpleKeeps the previous fixed DHW distribution and storage assumptions.
--dhw-sizing-mode check | size_storage | size_power | autoSelects whether EN 12831-3 only checks the supplied system or sizes storage volume and/or reheating power.

Known Workflow Boundaries

  • EN 12831-3 is implemented for DHW energy needs and DHW system design sizing with storage switch points, time lag, effective reheating power and supply curves.
  • Project-specific direct-flow fixture sizing, declared storage standby losses and special tapping programs can be supplied from external references such as EN 806-3, EN 12897/product data or EN 13203-2 when those inputs are required.
  • EN 14511 / EN 14825 support product rating maps and part-load correction; the complete regulatory SCOP/SEER labelling procedure is not implemented.
  • EN 16798-13 is implemented for compression cooling generation in the examples; non-compression generators are not implemented.
  • The default cooling generation is in EN 16798-13, while heating and DHW generation remain in EN 15316-4-2.
